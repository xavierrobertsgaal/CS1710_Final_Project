A Citizen's Guide to Responsible AI

Today's powerful AI systems, and their successors on the horizon, may very well change everything. They promise to spur advances in medicine, boost productivity, and free humans from unfulfilling cognitive tasks.

Yet, powerful AI systems must be developed responsibly, as they have enormous costs and pose important societal risks. Just to name three:

  • Cutting-edge AI requires gargantuan amounts of carbon-intensive energy to train and use.
  • AI enables criminals to launch sophisticated cyberattacks at speed and scale.
  • Some types of AI, like powerful agents, may be able to plan and achieve undesirable goals and evade control by AI labs or policymakers.

Policymakers are already crafting regulations to shape the future of AI R&D, and some of these are ending up on the ballot. We believe citizens should be informed about ‚Äì and participate in ‚Äì public discussion to achieve the promises of AI while avoiding its perils.

Therefore, our aim in this project is to inform our parents, friends, members of the public, and non-technical policymakers about selected, salient, and severe risks of AI.
