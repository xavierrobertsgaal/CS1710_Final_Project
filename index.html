A Citizen's Guide to Responsible AI

Today's AI systems offer unprecedented capabilities while introducing new risks.

This guide explores AI's environmental footprint, security risks, and autonomy concerns through data-driven visualizations.

AI's Hidden Environmental Cost

The environmental cost of AI can come both from the process of training AI models and from the public's daily use of AI.

Training AI models requires massive computational power, leading to substantial carbon emissions. AI-intensive tasks consistently produce more carbon emissions than traditional computing tasks.

Data Center Carbon Impact

Data centers training AI systems consume more power annually than the average American household will for 130 years.. These AI data centers contribute to the outsized power consumption of some states, like California and Texas.

However, most AI companies do not disclose the carbon impact of training their models. More transparency is needed.

Raise awareness on the power consumption of data centers and push for sustainability regulations.

Rising Energy Demand

AI energy consumption has increased since 2010, far outpacing efficiency improvements.

Without intervention, AI's energy consumption could exceed that of many countries within the next decade.

Advocate for green AI infrastructure in your organization and support initiatives promoting renewable energy in data centers.

AI Security Incidents

High-severity AI security incidents are now a feature of everyday life, coinciding with the widespread adoption of generative AI. These security incidences can range from minor annoyances to severe harm, resulting in tremendous financial loss.

As you can see by extending the area chart back to 1984 when data was first collected, the number of AI-related security incidences per year was negligible until after 2010, when AI first became prominent.

Not all sectors of the economy face the same risks. Explore how distinct AI threats have disproportionately impacted different areas of society.

Push for regular security audits of AI systems in your workplace and support comprehensive AI security policies. Inform loved ones about AI security risks such as AI-generated deepfakes and robocalls.

AI's Rapid Evolution

AI performance on software engineering tasks has improved dramatically. The latest models achieve scores that seemed impossible months ago on SWE-Bench, a standardized test of software understanding.

Recent scores above 50 represent a fundamental shift: AI systems can now handle complex programming tasks with human-like comprehension. This rate of improvement far exceeds historical trends in technology development.

We're approaching a potential inflection point where AI systems could significantly augment or automate aspects of AI development itself. This self-reinforcing cycle raises urgent questions about control and safety.

Join AI safety discussions in your community and support policies that promote responsible AI development practices.

Key Takeaways

Environmental Impact

AI environmental cost outpaces that of traditional computing tasks. Most data centers use lots of energy and rely on fossil fuels.

Support renewable energy use in AI infrastructure and be mindful of your own energy consumption.

Security Concerns

AI security incidents are now a feature of everyday life, and can be severely harmful.

Advocate for robust security measures in AI systems. Inform loved ones about AI security risks such as AI-generated deepfakes and robocalls.

Rapid Progress

AI capabilities are advancing at unprecedented rates, approaching a potential inflection point in self-improvement.

Get involved in AI safety discussions and support policies promoting responsible AI development.
